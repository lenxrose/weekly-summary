Recommended Next Actions

01

Require 3+ frames before assigning a person ID

Sub-2-second ghost events account for 54–62% of all raw IDs. Raising the confidence threshold to 3 frames eliminates most noise at the source and will significantly improve fragmentation ratios.

02

Improve cross-camera re-identification

Fragmentation of 3.7–4.6× means shoppers are counted multiple times. Scenario B (Incremental Actors) showed improving shopper counts as actor numbers grew (70 → 29 closer to GT 36), but re-ID accuracy needs to reach 1× before the Stress Test can be reliably evaluated at full group density.

03

Expand destination zone polygons — critical for Scenario A (Stress Test)

Under high group density (Scenario A), 0% of expected paths were detected. Group occlusion and shelf crowding may be causing attribution failures. Wider zone polygons and nearest-neighbour fallback would help recover detections when groups overlap at shelf boundaries.

04

Retrain emotion model and verify gaze hardware

Both are disabled for reporting. Gaze camera orientation and lighting should be verified before re-running either scenario — attention data is especially important for the Stress Test (Scenario A) where group shelf dwell behaviour is the core signal.
